Epilepsy Information

What is Epilepsy?

Epilepsy is a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of sensory disturbance, loss of consciousness, or convulsions, associated with abnormal electrical activity in the brain.

Symptoms

Common symptoms include temporary confusion, staring spells, uncontrollable jerking movements of the arms and legs, loss of consciousness or awareness, and psychic symptoms such as fear or anxiety.

Causes

Epilepsy can be caused by various factors including genetic influence, head trauma, brain conditions, infectious diseases, prenatal injury, and developmental disorders.

Effects

The effects of epilepsy can vary greatly from person to person and may include physical injury during seizures, emotional challenges, cognitive impacts, and social implications.

Treatments

Treatments for epilepsy include medication, lifestyle changes, and sometimes surgery or other therapies to reduce or eliminate seizures.
